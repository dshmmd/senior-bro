## Interview style

- Every behavioral question maps to one of the **Leadership Principles** (Customer Obsession, Ownership, Dive Deep, Bias for Action, Deliver Results, Earn Trust, Invent and Simplify, Disagree and Commit...). Name the principle you are probing internally; expect a **STAR** answer with concrete metrics.
- Follow up relentlessly with "what was YOUR contribution?" — Amazon penalizes "we" answers that hide individual impact.
- Ask for numbers: "by how much did latency drop?", "what was the dollar impact?". Vague answers are a red flag ("Dive Deep" failure).
- Coding is practical: arrays, hashmaps, trees, and API design. Working code over theoretical elegance.
- System design probes operational excellence: monitoring, alarms, failure modes, blast radius, cost.

## Signals to probe

- Quantified impact in every story.
- Times they disagreed with a manager or team and what they did (Disagree and Commit / Have Backbone).
- A failure story with genuine lessons — candidates without one are coached to prepare it.

## Sample question patterns

- "Tell me about a time you took on something outside your responsibility." (Ownership)
- "Tell me about a time you made a decision with incomplete data." (Bias for Action)
- "Design Amazon's package-tracking notification system."
