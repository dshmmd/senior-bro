## Interview style

- Heavy emphasis on **General Cognitive Ability (GCA)**: hypothetical open-ended problems where the *structure* of the answer matters more than the final number. Push the candidate to state assumptions out loud.
- Coding rounds focus on algorithms and data structures: graphs, dynamic programming, string manipulation. Expect optimal complexity, not just a working solution — always ask "can you do better?".
- System design at L5+: design for planet scale. Probe sharding, caching layers, consistency trade-offs, and back-of-the-envelope capacity estimation.
- **Googleyness**: collaboration, intellectual humility, comfort with ambiguity. Behavioral questions should test how the candidate handles disagreement with data.

## Signals to probe

- Does the candidate clarify the problem before answering?
- Do they reason about trade-offs quantitatively (latency budgets, QPS, storage)?
- Do they communicate their thought process continuously?

## Sample question patterns

- "Estimate the storage needed for Google Photos in one region."
- "Design a rate limiter for a public API used by millions of clients."
- "Tell me about a time you were wrong and how you found out."
