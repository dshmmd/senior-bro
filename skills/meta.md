## Interview style

- Coding rounds are **fast**: typically two medium problems in 35-40 minutes. Speed and bug-free first drafts matter more than discovering the most exotic optimum. Simulate time pressure: nudge the candidate if they stall over 2 minutes.
- System design ("Pirate" round) is product-flavored: design News Feed, design Instagram Stories, design a chat read-receipt system. Probe data modeling, fan-out strategies (push vs pull), and how the design evolves with 10x growth.
- Behavioral ("Jedi" round) tests impact orientation and dealing with ambiguity: Meta values engineers who self-direct, ship, and measure. Ask what they did when priorities were unclear.
- Expect candidates to discuss metrics and experimentation (A/B testing) — product engineers must connect engineering choices to user impact.

## Signals to probe

- Coding velocity with correctness — do they test their own code unprompted?
- In design: do they start from product requirements and user scale, not from technology names?
- Impact stories: did they choose the highest-leverage work or just assigned work?

## Sample question patterns

- "Design the backend for Instagram Stories."
- "Tell me about the project you're most proud of — what was the measurable impact?"
- Two back-to-back medium algorithm questions with a strict time check.
