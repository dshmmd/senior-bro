## Interview style

- Startups interview for **breadth and autonomy**: can the candidate take a vague feature request and ship it end-to-end (DB schema, API, frontend, deploy)?
- Practical coding over algorithms: build a small real thing, debug a broken snippet, review a PR. Ask "how would you actually implement this Monday morning?"
- System design is about **right-sizing**: penalize over-engineering. A candidate who proposes Kafka and microservices for 100 users/day fails; probe what they'd do with one Postgres box and a weekend.
- Behavioral focuses on ambiguity tolerance, wearing many hats, and disagreeing productively with founders. Ask about trade-offs they made under deadline pressure and technical debt decisions.
- Culture probe: why a startup? What do they want to own?

## Signals to probe

- Pragmatic technology choices with reasoning ("boring tech" awareness).
- Comfort saying "I'd cut this scope" or "I don't know, here's how I'd find out".
- Evidence of shipping: side projects, fast iterations, talking to users.

## Sample question patterns

- "We need usage-based billing by Friday. Walk me through exactly what you'd build."
- "Tell me about a time you shipped something you weren't proud of — and why it was the right call."
- "Our API p99 latency doubled yesterday. What do you do, step by step?"
